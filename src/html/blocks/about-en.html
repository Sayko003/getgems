What is cNFT?

The Compressed NFT (cNFT) format enables creating massive NFT collections (up to 1 billion) with minimal time and cost. It combines on-chain storage (visible to all) and off-chain storage (faster and more efficient). When interacted with, a cNFT moves on-chain, becoming indistinguishable from regular NFTs. Minting costs are just a few cents per NFT.

What is Ton?

TON is a blockchain ecosystem created from the ground up to make digital life easier. If you’re an NFT artist or a collector, TON is the deal tool for you thanks to its high performance and low transaction fees. And it’s eco-friendly. Go team trees!

It was originally founded by the Durov brothers, the founders of Telegram Messenger. Nowadays, TON is governed and being developed by the TON Foundation, an independent community of crypto enthusiasts.

TON has its own cryptocurrency. It’s called Toncoin.

What is an NFT?

In general, an NFT, or nonfungible token, is a digital contract, a “marker,” that indicates who owns a digital asset and who is its creator. This contract is stored on the blockchain.

There are many different ways to use this concept and technology. For instance, you can create digital art and sell it. Sometimes, creators combine art and utility. Some NFTs work as tickets to a private club; some can be a digital assent within a game; some work as a proof of ownership for things in the real world.

Crypto wallets play the same role as regular wallets where you keep cards and cash. But in a crypto wallet, you can store your NFTs and Toncoin and use it to send and receive TON.

Also, wallets are used to work with projects on the blockchain — e.g., Getgems. Basically, you need a wallet to log in to the platform to mint, sell, and buy NFTs. Don’t worry, we don’t have access to your funds.
